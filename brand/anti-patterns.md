# Anti-patterns

What Celeste actively avoids — in words and visuals. Drop any of these and the brand collapses into
something generic.

## In words

- Spiritual wellness language
- Generic stress/self-care routine messaging
- Hero ingredient stories
- Static skin type thinking
- Em dashes
- Writing like a spiritual wellness brand, an empowerment brand, a clinical dermatology brand, or
  generic clean beauty

## In visuals — general

- Soft-focus botanical aesthetics
- Pastel minimalism that signals luxury without saying anything
- Whitewashed casting
- Clinical or cold lighting
- Staged, sterile, or overly styled compositions
- Anything that reads as stock photography

## In visuals — people photography

- Full face portraits without product
- Blurred or bokeh backgrounds, shallow depth of field
- White studio backgrounds
- Prop styling of any kind
- Glowy, perfected skin with no texture
- Circular product application that reads as face wash
- Glittery or shimmery product
- Unrealistic product amounts
- Overly symmetrical, airbrushed, or identical-looking faces
- Portraits where product is secondary to the person
- Clinical, cold, or overly bright skin rendering

## In visuals — product photography

- Cool or blue-toned light
- Hard-edged, patterned, or graphic shadows (only a single soft-edged shadow grounding the product)
- Glossy tube finish on the body (cap stays polished chrome by design)
- Unrealistic product textures
- Dark or saturated backgrounds that compete with product or formula color
- Clinical white backgrounds
- Dramatic shadows that obscure product texture or label legibility
- Label text or wordmark appearing pasted, flat, or uncommitted to the tube's curved surface
- AI defaults: over-polished, too-perfect, stock-feeling compositions

## Why this matters

Celeste's core idea only works if all three pillars hold at once: the cycle insight, the
platform+product system, and the warm-but-specific tone. Drop any one and it becomes a wellness app
with no product, a skincare line with no intelligence, or a novelty that doesn't earn its
seriousness.
