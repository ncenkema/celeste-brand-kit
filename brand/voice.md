# Voice

## The one-line description

Celeste is both the intelligence and the product — the platform that knows your phase, and the
skincare formulated for it. The voice is warm, intimate, cerebral, understated, friendly-expert,
lifestyle-led, genuine, grounded, body-literate.

## Tone keywords

Warm, intimate, responsive, future-facing, body-literate, honest, understated, cerebral, sensory,
friendly-expert, lifestyle-led, genuine, grounded.

## What copy should do

- Make women feel seen — like someone finally built skincare around how their body actually works.
- Lead with biological truth, land with feeling.
- Speak to the specific, lived experience of having a cycle: the breakout that arrives on schedule,
  the week your skin suddenly feels like someone else's.
- Treat the cycle as biological reality, not a wellness trend.
- Position adaptability as the breakthrough — not an ingredient, not a miracle.
- Stay understated. Never announce itself, never perform, let the intelligence speak.
- Feel specific to Celeste. If a line could belong to any other skincare brand, rewrite it.

## Never write like

A spiritual wellness brand, an empowerment brand, a clinical dermatology brand, or generic clean
beauty. Never produce generic, interchangeable copy — if it could have been written by AI for any
wellness brand, it isn't good enough.

## Reference vibe

Ritual's flexibility, cycle-tracking app intelligence, EADEM's editorial warmth, Rhode's skin-first
simplicity, the precision of a dermatology brand, the intimacy of the friend who texts you what
phase she's in.

## Hard rules

- No em dashes. Use a comma, a period, or rewrite the sentence.

## Register by surface

| Surface | Register |
| --- | --- |
| Tube / packaging label | Technical, minimal — phase name + number only (DM Mono) |
| Billboard | Short, plain, declarative ("skincare made to cycle with you") |
| Product copy | Warm, intimate, understated, body-literate |
| Governance / process docs | Plain and honest |
