# Photography — Product

## Two image modes

This covers two distinct categories of Celeste product imagery:

1. **Texture and Formula References** — unbranded, close-up shots used to study and lock in how
   each formula behaves.
2. **Finished Product Shots** — fully branded, upright tube photography used for packaging, ads, and
   the brand system.

Lighting, focus, and tone rules (see `treatment.md`) apply to both. Composition and branding rules
differ, noted below.

## Composition — texture and formula references

Tubes lay flat or at angles on the surface, caps off, radiating outward from center. Product
extrudes naturally from each opening and rests on the surface. Arrangement should feel slightly
undone, not perfectly styled. ECU or BCU framing — tight enough that tubes fill the frame and surface
becomes secondary.

## Composition — finished product shots

Tube stands upright, cap at bottom, full tube visible from cap to crimped top. Centered in frame with
even breathing room on all sides, or arranged at a slight diagonal for editorial energy. Full label
system visible and legible — wordmark, technical text, and phase indicator all rendered as part of
the tube surface, never pasted on top.

## Surface

Default warm neutral. Matte, flat, no reflections. Surface disappears behind the product — it is
ground, not subject. Backgrounds may be this warm neutral surface, or a solid monochrome color
tonally related to that product's own hex value — used for single-product social and ad imagery.
Never clinical white; never dark or saturated backgrounds that absorb formula or tube color.

## Avoid (product-specific)

- Cool or blue-toned light
- Soft or blurred backgrounds, shallow depth of field
- Clinical or sterile compositions
- Anything that reads as stock photography
- Glossy tube finish on the body (cap remains polished chrome by design)
- Unrealistic product textures
- Dark or saturated backgrounds that compete with product or formula color
- Clinical white backgrounds
- Dramatic shadows that obscure product texture or label legibility
- Label text or wordmark appearing pasted, flat, or uncommitted to the tube's curved surface
- AI defaults: over-polished, too-perfect, stock-feeling compositions

See `identity.md` for the tube system and formula details, `treatment.md` for shared lighting/focus/
tone rules.
