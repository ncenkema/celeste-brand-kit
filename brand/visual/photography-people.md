# Photography — People

## Who

Women 25–40. Diverse range of ages, ethnicities, skin types, and face structures. Real skin —
visible pores, freckles, texture, variation. Not retouched, not symmetrical, not decorative. Models
feel like real women, not cast talent or aspirational archetypes. Varied eye color, brow shape, skin
tone across outputs — no copy-paste faces. Bare faced, no makeup. Skin is the subject as it naturally
is.

## Crop and framing

- Never a full face portrait.
- Tight crop: half face, eyes only, jawline, neck, forearm, shoulder, collarbone.
- Person can be nearly anonymous. Body parts as landscape.
- ECU or BCU framing.

## Expression

Quiet relief, calm, understated ease. She knows her skin, she trusts her routine. Not performed
happiness. Not blank neutrality. Not sad or bored. Eyes open or closed: variety encouraged. Vary face
angle and gaze direction.

## Product

Product is always present and central. Never incidental or secondary. The product-on-skin moment is
the subject: the swipe, the drop, the application. Directional swipe, serum drop, or formula resting
on skin — never circular or face-wash-like. Realistic amount of product: a swipe or drop, not a
palmful. Realistic hand positioning: natural, functional, not staged. Hands and fingers in frame when
relevant. Product reads as skincare, not makeup — no glitter, shimmer, or highlighter effect.

## Skin

Skin texture is realistic: freckles and pores present but natural, not exaggerated. Warmth comes from
skin undertones, not lighting color. Skin should feel alive and present, not tired, dim, or overly
glowy.

## Avoid (people-specific)

- Full face portraits without product
- Blurred or bokeh backgrounds
- White studio backgrounds
- Prop styling of any kind
- Soft-focus botanical aesthetics
- Glowy perfected skin with no texture
- Circular product application that reads as face wash
- Glittery or shimmery product
- Unrealistic product amounts
- Overly symmetrical, airbrushed, or identical-looking faces
- Whitewashed or non-diverse casting
- Portraits where product is secondary to the person
- Clinical, cold, or overly bright skin rendering

See `treatment.md` for shared lighting/focus/tone rules.
