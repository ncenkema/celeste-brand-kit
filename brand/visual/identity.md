# Visual Identity

## Overall direction

Warm, editorial, sensory, slightly undone — never clinical, never soft-focus botanical default.
Macro texture, realistic placement and dispersing of product, intimate but systematic.

## The tube system

All four products share an identical tube form and cap. Color is the primary differentiator between
phases, alongside the label system below.

- **Shape:** matte satin-finish tube, tapered cylindrical body, flat sealed crimp at the top,
  rounded shoulders tapering to the neck.
- **Cap:** polished chrome sphere cap with a silver metal collar. High shine, reflective,
  mirror-like finish — consistent across all four products.

## The label system

Every finished product shot includes the full label system, rendered as if printed or embossed
directly onto the tube surface — never as a flat pasted sticker.

- **Wordmark:** "Celeste" in cursive script (Cedarville Cursive), centered on the tube face, running
  vertically with the tube's form. This is the only context where the cursive wordmark is used — it
  is never used as a headline or body font elsewhere in the brand system.
- **Technical text:** set in DM Mono, positioned at the top of the tube face. Reads as product
  number and name on the first line, phase name on the second line — e.g. "03 - OPAL" /
  "OVULATORY PHASE."
- **Phase indicator:** four small circles near the base of the tube. One filled circle marks the
  product's position in the four-phase cycle; the remaining three are unfilled outlines.

**Rule:** wordmark and technical text color always contrasts with the tube's own color for
legibility. Mend's tube is light (cream), so its text is dark (slate blue) — the only product where
text isn't white/cream. Float, Opal, and Lull all have deep/saturated tube colors, so their text
stays white/cream. Packaging boxes match this same tube color and text-color pairing exactly.

Packaging boxes carry the wordmark only (see `surfaces/packaging.md`), in the same tube color and
text-color pairing as above.

## Formula and texture (per product)

Each tube has a distinct formula, different in viscosity, color, opacity, and surface behavior.
Formulas must feel believable and appealing — never gross or intimidating. Formula colors must be
realistic and appetizing — warm, skin-adjacent, or naturally tinted, never artificial or neon.

| Product | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| Mend | Thick opaque white cream that coils and holds shape |
| Float | Clear gel with subtle texture and bubbling |
| Opal | Soft pink balm, smooth semi-opaque finish, labradorescent shift |
| Lull | Translucent amber oil that spreads with slight shimmer |

See `treatment.md` for lighting/focus/tone rules and `photography-product.md` for full composition
rules across the two image modes (texture/formula references vs. finished product shots).
