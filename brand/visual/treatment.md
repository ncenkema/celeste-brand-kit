# Treatment

These rules apply to **all** Celeste imagery — branded and unbranded, product and people.

## Lighting

Warm and directional from one side. Warmth comes from the subject itself — skin undertones, product
color, surface tone, tube finish — never from an amber filter or forced golden glow. Never cool,
flat, or studio-clinical.

For finished product shots specifically: lighting should feel observed and natural — soft, diffused,
gently directional, rather than a bright commercial studio strobe. No hard-edged, patterned, or
graphic shadows; only a single soft-edged shadow grounding the product.

This is a governance-level rule — fixed once in this file, not patched prompt by prompt. A prior
error where "soft window light" produced hard-edged blind shadows was corrected at the source
document, and the fix held across every subsequent generation.

## Focus

Sharp across the entire frame. No bokeh. No blurred backgrounds. No shallow depth of field. Every
detail must read clearly — skin texture, product detail, label text, wordmark.

## Tone

Intimate, material, grounded. Images should feel observed, not staged. Never stock. Never dramatic.
Never over-lit.

## Always avoid

Cool or blue-toned light, soft or blurred backgrounds, clinical or sterile compositions, anything
that reads as stock photography, harsh bright studio lighting, glossy specular highlights that feel
commercial rather than observed.

See `photography-product.md` and `photography-people.md` for mode-specific composition rules, and
`anti-patterns.md` for the full avoid list.
