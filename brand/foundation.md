# Foundation

The bedrock of Celeste. Everything else in `brand/` answers to this file.

## One-liner

**Celeste — skincare made to cycle with you.**

## Mission

- Build the first skin health solution that moves with every woman, her cycle, and her life stage.
- A platform and skincare line built in tandem. The intelligence and the product, together.
- A skincare partner that knows your luteal phase from your follicular and formulates accordingly.

## Vision

- Skincare that finally knows your body as well as you do.
- A skincare partner created to adapt with your cycle. A fluid, flexible system that moves with you,
  week by week, phase by phase.

## Background

**What:** A cycle-synced skincare platform. Four phase-specific products tailored to where you are
in your menstrual cycle.

**Why:** Skincare treats skin like it's static, but it's not — it changes throughout your cycle. No
brand really leans into that, and it's a gap worth designing into.

Skincare is overwhelming. Hundreds of products, routines, and conflicting methods compete for your
bathroom shelf and your attention. And yet, with all that noise, the industry has missed something
fundamental: your skin is not the same throughout your cycle. It shifts, reacts, glows, and
struggles in direct response to your hormonal cycle.

**Scope note:** Celeste was originally envisioned with a platform component that pairs with any
cycle-tracking app you already use. This project focused primarily on the product side — the
platform/app pairing remains a direction to push further.

## Positioning statement

For women 25–40 who track their cycle, care about hormone health, and are tired of skincare that
treats them like a static skin type, Celeste is the first skin health solution built entirely around
the hormonal cycle — where a platform and a product line work as one system. Unlike generic
skincare that assumes one skin type or one routine forever, Celeste treats your skin as something
that changes on a schedule, and meets it there.

## The market gap (our core provocation)

Three things are true at once, and no one else is holding all three:

- **Current skincare practices are rigid.** Built for fixed skin types and mass audiences, missing
  how much skin fluctuates across the cycle. Skin on day 5 is a different organ than skin on day 20.
- **Nothing out there is built to move with your cycle.** A few brands have noticed the connection
  between hormones and skin, but noticing it and building around it are two different things. What
  exists today is either a platform with no products, or a product with no intelligence behind it.
- **Celeste is the first to build the whole thing.** A platform that knows where you are in your
  cycle, and a product line formulated to meet you there. Both. Together. Finally.

Drop any one of these and Celeste becomes something we refuse to be (see
[`anti-patterns.md`](anti-patterns.md)): a wellness app with no product, a skincare line with no
intelligence, or a novelty tied to a trend.

## The four-phase system

Celeste fields **four products, one per cycle phase**: **Mend** (01, Menstrual), **Float** (02,
Follicular), **Opal** (03, Ovulatory), **Lull** (04, Luteal). Each carries its own phase color and
formulation, unified by one tube system and one wordmark. See [`visual/identity.md`](visual/identity.md)
for the tube ↔ phase details.

## On the name

Celeste was built to feel personal, feminine, and quietly cyclical. It hints at "cyclical" without
being too lunar or on-the-nose about the syncing — celestial, but not heavy-handed. The goal was
confident, understated, familiar: the older sister who has everything figured out, not a name that
announces itself.

## What we are / are not

| We are | We are not |
| --- | --- |
| A platform + product system, together | A wellness app with no product |
| Built around a real biological rhythm | A skincare line with one static routine |
| Warm, intelligent, editorial | Clinical, sterile, spiritual/holistic |
| Specific to the cycle | Undifferentiated "wellness" |
